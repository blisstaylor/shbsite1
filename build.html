1928

This photograph, produced on 21 August 1928, is a view of the southern pylon of the Sydney Harbour Bridge while under construction. Two vehicular ferries on the Blues Point service can be seen as well as the Dawes Point ferry ramp. In the foreground, a steam hopper barge of the Sydney Harbour Trust is inbound having dumped her load of spoil at sea.

1930

This image from a glass plate negative, produced in about March or April 1930, shows the Sydney Harbour Bridge under construction with the two arches nearing connection and the stone pylons incomplete. The photograph was take from Lavender Bay on the North Shore and in foreground are the dual railway lines that lead to the North Shore terminus and replacement Milsons Point wharf. The ferry 'Kuttabul' is approaching this wharf. The wharf pontoon still exists (2016) and was used for many years at the Luna Park fun fair park. At various times it was used for dodgem racing and as a dance hall. The electric trains seen in the image were known as 'red rattlers'. The railway tracks are still used as 'lay-bys' for off-peak train sets.
