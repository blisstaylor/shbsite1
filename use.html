1932 – 2022

1968

Negatives of P & O liner Canberra by David Mist, 1968

Since its construction in 1932, the scale and location of the Sydney Harbour Bridge saw it become a national and international icon of Australia symbolising the gateway to the nation's emerging cosmopolitan aspirations.

1993

Colour transparencies (6), for cover of Telecom white pages, film, David Mist, Sydney, 1993. Three models in evening wear with Sydney Opera House and Sydney Harbour Bridge in the background, fashion by Studibaker Hawke.

Collection of transparencies depicting Telecom white pages photographed by David Mist

1982

Sydney Harbour Bridge poster (1982 50th anniversary reissued edition)

Collaboration between commercial artist Arthur Whitmore and graphic designer Douglas Annand resulted as a consequence of the design competition in which the judges admired elements of each artist's competition entry, inviting them to collaborate on the final design. The illustration of the people in the foreground is attributed to Arthur 'Bob' Whitmore.

1998 – 1999

Storyboard, consisting of a colour print mounted on cardboard backing. Opening Ceremony "Eternity" segment original concept drawings. Black text below the picture "The Sydney Harbour Bridge erupts into fireworks". Handwritten in black marker pen on the back of the storyboard "B33 20/1".

Fireworks and the Sydney Habour Bridge have become synonymous today.

Storyboard, 'Sydney Olympic 2000 Opening Ceremony', paper, designed by Ric Birch, Sydney, c. 1998-1999
